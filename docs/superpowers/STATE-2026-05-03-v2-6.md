## What's NOT WORKING (next-session blocker)

### mapId=24 mapping mismatch

Symptom in V2.6.3 run:
- Party reaches `Indoors(mapId=24, localX=3, localY=2)` after Coneria Town exit.
- ExitInterior calls `findPathToExit` → BLOCKED.
- Offline ROM dump confirms tile at (3, 2) in InteriorMap[24] = byte 0x3c (padding).
- All 4 neighbours also 0x3c. BFS can't expand.

Two hypotheses (need verification):
1. **RAM `currentMapId` byte (0x0048) ≠ InteriorMapLoader index.** FF1 may use
   a different table or apply a transform. Loader.load(N) might decode wrong map.
2. **localX/localY are scrolled-screen-relative, not map-relative.** FF1 sub-maps
   might use a viewport offset where party at "(3, 2)" means "3 tiles right of
   scroll origin, which is at tile (40, 12) in ROM data".

Either way, the next session needs to:
- Reverse-engineer FF1 sub-map indexing (look at `knes-debug/resources/profiles/ff1.json`
  for known RAM fields; compare with FF1 community RAM maps).
- Add a screenshot at the stuck moment to visually confirm what room party is in.
- Consider hardcoded mapping table for known sub-map IDs.

### Coneria entry trigger at (145, 152) is byte-invisible

V2.6.0/V2.6.3 evidence: party at (145, 152) on overworld, step S → transported
to mapId=8. The byte at the stepped-onto tile (145, 153) is 0x76 (regular
grass, not TOWN/CASTLE). FF1 has separate teleport tables in ROM that aren't
captured by tile-byte classification. V2.5.2 abort + V2.5.9 fog defensive
gracefully recover, so this is **not blocking**, but the agent can't
*proactively* avoid these tiles.

Next-session optional: decode FF1's teleport tables from ROM and add as a
location-based override layer in OverworldMap.
